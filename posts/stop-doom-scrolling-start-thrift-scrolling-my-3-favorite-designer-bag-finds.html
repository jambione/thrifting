Stop Doom Scrolling, Start Thrift Scrolling: My 3 Favorite Designer Bag Finds

kara July 21, 2026

Do you doom scroll? Many people do: spending time scrolling through social media or news apps, reading all of the bad news in the world, and feeling depressed about it.

Start thrift scrolling instead. When you swap headlines for online secondhand stores, you start discovering incredible designers you never knew existed. Some of my absolute favorite pieces in my closet are from brands I had never heard of until I started thrifting.

A quick caveat: my recent Issey Miyake Bao Bao Lucent Tote is not on this list. I have already shared that story, so consider this an ode to my top three favorite thrifted bags other than my Issey Miyake.

Find #1: The Buttery Leather Vintage Bag (Furla)

My thrift scrolling journey really started in 2021. I had gained about 35 pounds after ankle surgery in 2020, and I felt depressed about shopping in local stores. I did not want to spend a lot of money on new clothes either. I ended up buying a few clothes, but no handbags.

Then I decided to sell some of my older, smaller clothes on ThredUp. I had ThredUp credit from those sales, and I decided I wanted to buy a vintage 100% leather handbag. I found one I liked, but I had never heard of the company. I bought it anyway.

It turns out that company was Furla, an Italian leather goods brand in business since 1927. When the bag arrived, I loved the soft, buttery, but structured leather. Since then, I have acquired two more Furla handbags, including one I bought at the Furla store in Venice in 2024. That was my first time going into a designer store, and even crazier, it was my first time buying a handbag that was not on sale.

Find #2: The 2000s "It" Bag (Chloé Paddington)

In April 2022, I went on a hunt for a really cool leather handbag. I told my husband I wanted one for Mother’s Day, and he was fine with me shopping around. I searched for about three months, well after Mother’s Day, to find the right piece.

I finally found a gold leather bag on The RealReal with a heavy, cute padlock. I had heard of Chloé, but I did not know much about the brand until I saw this piece. The style was called the Paddington, and it was the "It" bag of 2006.

At $115, it felt like a lot of money for a used handbag, but it looked so awesome. After reading on Reddit about counterfeit bags, I got nervous and paid $30 for an authentication service. The good news: it was real, and it was a rare colorway that originally retailed in 2006 for $1,480.

About a month later, I found an original sale ticket in one of the interior pockets showing it had been marked down from $1,400 to $1,200. I guess I could have saved myself $30 on that appraisal.

Interestingly enough, Chloé just re-released the Paddington in time for its 20th anniversary. I might just start carrying mine around more often now. Style really is cyclical!

Find #3: The "Fun in Functional" Bag (Longchamp Le Pliage Neo)

I know I said in another post not to thrift Longchamp, but I have managed to score two different Le Pliage bags in limited edition patterns by thrifting.

In 2023, I was looking for a waterproof crossbody bag and found a Longchamp Le Pliage Neo from the Fantaisie line (2016-2017) on ThredUp for $42. It puts the fun in functional (is that a mom joke? Sorry). Every time I need a lightweight, hands-free bag that can handle the rain, I grab this one. It is easily one of my most practical favorite finds.

Since then, I have acquired 4 more Longchamp bags (3 were gifts, 1 was a thrift) - they are total workhorses when it comes to travel and weather.

Happy Thrifting!

Next time you catch yourself mindlessly scrolling through stressful news, open up your favorite resale site instead. You never know what awesome piece of fashion history you will discover tucked away on a digital rack.
