What I wore - June 24, 2026

kara June 24, 2026

Nothing says summer like a dress and sneakers, right? (Except instead of showing this awesome outfit in my bedroom, I asked Canva AI to put me on a New England street. But it chose autumn because my prompt wasn’t specific enough, and I ran out of AI credits. Dang it all.) The monochromatic look has really been resonating with me recently.  I’ll break down this look.

The dress: a ThredUp find, Eileen Fisher. It was a severely high-low look, which I actually dislike. I ended up hemming the back so it’s the same length as the front (thank you, sewing machine!). It’s a boat-neck style with cap sleeves, which I dig. The dress is actually a little bit too big on me now, but I can cover up those extra loose armholes with a snazzy white jacket.

The jacket: a local find, new with tags Abercrombie and Fitch from Uptown Consignment, for about $25. This jacket is really quite tailored and high quality. The tag said it was being sold for 100 Euros… which is a little confusing to me. The original owner must have bought it in Europe (I didn’t know A&F had stores in Europe, but Gemini tells me that they have stores in the UK, Germany, and France), brought it home, and never wore it. But that suits me just fine (see what I did there?).

The handbag: a Tory Burch ThredUp find from about 5 years ago, when I first tried ThredUp. This bag was a surprise, actually. It said it was in “Good” condition, but it arrived in a dust bag, with tags on it, and not a scratch to be had. I was thrilled to find it. I always get compliments when I use this bag, it’s so bright and cheerful.

The shoes: these weren’t thrifted, but a great Amazon deal, and the most comfortable casual (non-running) sneakers I have. They’re Cole Haan Grand Crosscourt Joy Platform and I bought them for about $75. This last year’s design, however, so right now they are on sale for $60. I’ve seen these new on Poshmark though, for about $40.

The jewelry: I don’t thrift jewelry enough. I am a big fan of Etsy (I even sell my own jewelry), and I believe I bought the earring studs (you can’t see them, but they are small crosses) on Caitlyn Minimalist, and the necklace I bought 3 years ago on Amazon (you can still buy it today for $35). 

This outfit is polished but super comfortable. I would wear this to my office, or around the house without the jacket (like I am today) and just throw the jacket on if I got chilly.

And that’s my outfit of the day!
