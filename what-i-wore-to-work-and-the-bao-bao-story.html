What I wore to work (and the Bao Bao story)

kara June 26, 2026

I'm probably one of the only people in the world who loves going to work on a Friday. I think it's because I'm naturally an introvert, and the office is silent on Fridays. My colleague and I hang out in a conference room and have fantastic ideas on how to make the technology at our company work better for us. Well, some of the time. A lot of the time we sit on meetings and politely object to things that don't make sense, or explain what it is we plan to do if someone doesn't interrupt us and ask us to fix something immediately broken.

That said, dressing for the office can be daunting, right? Especially in the summer. Not so! Thrifting makes it cheap and easy, and you can bring it all together with a cool handbag. Enter my beautiful Issey Miyake Bao Bao bag I got in Kyoto. There's actually a fun story that goes along with the bag, but first I'll break down my outfit.

The shirt is Lavia 18, which is an off-shoot of the company Piazza Sempione, an Italian company based in Milan. ThredUp find, $12. I'm not super wild about this top to be honest, because it's completely transparent, so I have to wear a tank beneath it. It has nice texture, though, and I feel like that plays nicely with plain linen pants.

The pants are wide leg linen trousers from Quince. I am obsessed with Quince. If you haven't heard of it, go check it out, really nice basics at very high quality for low prices. But how low is low? I bought these pants off Poshmark, new with tags, from a company called (re)vive. I almost don't want to tell people about it to keep it a secret, but they sell overstocks on Poshmark for really cheap. These pants were $10.

The shoes are Ancient Greek Sandals, which I bought on ThredUp for $27. This seemed expensive to me for a thrift price for a company I'd never heard of, until I got the shoes. They are super high quality. I thought, I wonder how much these go for... and looked at their website. Wow! Shoes like these retail for about $400. They're all leather, all handmade in Greece. 

The bag, oh the beloved bag. I have been eyeing Issey Miyake Bao Bao bags for a long time now. They are SO COOL. Lucent Matte retails for about $700 on their website, if you can find it. On ThredUp, they go for between $250-350. I see Nordstrom is selling a shinier one on sale for $500, and Issey Miyake's site has a neat magenta shiny one for $400 on sale. Obviously, I would never spend that on a handbag. So I was determined to find one on the cheap when I went to Japan.

My friend Junko was very kind when told her about my quest for the Bao Bao Lucent tote, and indulged me by letting me drag her all around Dotonburi Street in Hiroshima looking for a second hand store. I finally on my own went to a shopping mall in Hiroshima, found a second hand designer threads store called Ragtag, and asked if they had one. Yes, they did, in their warehouse, and they shipped it to Kyoto where I was going in 5 days (because he said it takes 3-5 days to ship it to a store from the warehouse). 

I went to the store in Kyoto to pick it up (an adventure, since I went alone). It had some marks on the outside, a "B" rating which means, essentially, it has some wear. The inside was pristine, however. It was 28,000 yen. I said yes, I'll buy it. He actually gave me a discount with my passport to avoid paying the tax, so it was more like 26,000 yen. I didn't know how much that was, but I was thrilled to have my bag. 

The credit card statement says I spent $162 in Ragtag that day. And I was able, with a little dishsoap at the Airbnb, get off the marks. So I basically got a mint condition Bao Bao Lucent for a fraction of the cost. And funny thing is, when I got home, I saw on their global site under "Sold" that the actual bag would have been sold on their site for $250. 

Anyway, that's the story of the bag. Enjoy your weekend, everyone!
